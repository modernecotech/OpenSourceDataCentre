# Breaker and Fuse Coordination

Coordination proves the closest suitable protective device clears a fault before upstream devices trip unnecessarily.

Required study inputs:

- source fault current;
- converter, rectifier, BESS, and generator contribution;
- DC bus topology;
- cable lengths and impedances;
- breaker and fuse curves;
- contactor ratings;
- rack and row distribution;
- maintenance bypass modes.

Required outputs:

- time-current coordination curves;
- selectivity limits;
- device settings;
- fault scenarios;
- exceptions and mitigations;
- commissioning tests.

No DC-first design should be accepted without a protection and selectivity study by a qualified electrical engineer.
